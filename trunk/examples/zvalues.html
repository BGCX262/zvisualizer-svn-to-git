Positioning and assigning a z-index value to an HTML element creates a stacking context.
Use the following links to see what happens when the z-index of #div3 is changed.

No stacking context, the children of #div3 are stacked in parent context:
  • reset to no z-index specified
  • z-index: auto

Creates lower stacking context, send behind the green:
  • z-index: -1
  • z-index: 0
  • z-index: 1

Creates higher stacking context, send above the green:
  • z-index: 9

IE6 / IE7:

Merely having positioning creates a stacking context, so the children of #div3 are always stacked inside the #div3 context.
